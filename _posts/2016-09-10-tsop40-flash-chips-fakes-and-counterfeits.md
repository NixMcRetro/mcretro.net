![st-micro-m29f032d-tsop40](/assets/images/2016/img_0555.jpg)

Does the above image look like an AMD AM29F032B? Think again! Oh wait, the GQ-4X4 actually identifies it properly. It's an ST Micro M29F032D TSOP40 chip disguised as an AMD AM29F032B. Genius! Pin compatible so it doesn't affect me too much.

![amd-am29f032b-tsop40](/assets/images/2016/img_0554.jpg)

Above is the real McCoy. Genuine even down to the chip level, which was sort of expected given the chip is genuine and it's a chip... at any rate they both work well with the TSOP to Willem adapter for writing SNES ROMs via the GQ-4X / GQ-4X4 programmer. I don't know why the counterfeiters would even bother silkscreening AMD onto them, ST Micro isn't _that_ bad!
