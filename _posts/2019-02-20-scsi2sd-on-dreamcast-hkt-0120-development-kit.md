I ended up mounting the [SCSI2SD](http://www.codesrc.com/mediawiki/index.php?title=SCSI2SD) in my recently sold Sega Dreamcast Katana into a [3D printed sled](https://web.archive.org/web/20190217042332/https://68kmla.org/forums/index.php?/topic/24466-scsi2sd-mounting-bracket-for-se30/). I've just noticed now that there are plenty of caddies that help them fit into the space of a 3.5" hard drive. If I did it all again, I'd do it slightly differently now!

![](/assets/images/2019/img_0632.jpg)

I am happy to see that development of the SCSI2SD continues with them now using a full sized SD card and larger PCB. Makes sense in my eyes as it is replacing, in almost all cases, the form factor of a 3.5" hard drive - pretty darned roomy! On the topic of 50-pin SCSI though, old school Macs must love this solid state solution too. It's times like this I wish I had my old LC475, but then I remember how much space it took up and how brittle all the plastic was on it. One problem at a time!
