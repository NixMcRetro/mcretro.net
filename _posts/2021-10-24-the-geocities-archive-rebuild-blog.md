I've been working on dialling up on a MiSTer since April 2021. In the longer term I knew I would need websites that were small enough to browser over an old web browser. Enter [GeoCities](https://web.archive.org/web/19961022173245/http://www.geocities.com/). Deleted by Yahoo in 2009, [Archive Team](https://wiki.archiveteam.org/index.php/GeoCities_Project) saved a fair chunk of it. It's not all there but it's better than the absolutely nothing Yahoo has on offer.

As it happened [RetroChallenge](https://www.retrochallenge.org) 2021/10 was running, so in the 11th hour I threw my hat into the (web)ring. For the past several weeks I've been keeping [a blog of my progress](https://forum.acms.org.au/t/geocities-rebuild-for-older-browsers) on the newly launched Australian Computer Museum Society (ACMS) forum.

This has made things progress a lot faster than expected. I hadn't planned on starting this part of the project until next year. Yet here we are. Now I can browse the old internet through real modems connected to a MiSTer running Windows 98.

You can check out the 56k friendly GeoCities at [http://geocities.mcretro.net](/homepages/geocities). There's still a lot of work going on in the backend. Expect trouble! 🎃
