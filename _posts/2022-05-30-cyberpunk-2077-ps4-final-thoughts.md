Months have flown by on the calendar and I wanted to glance back on Cyberpunk 2077. Ahhh the memories. I remember reinstalling from disc back to the 1.00 unpatched release with no internet connected.

![](/assets/images/2022/img_0940.jpg)

It has been the only game that had me take my PlayStation completely offline to install the unpatched v1.00, from disc, in order to achieve a platinum trophy.

![](/assets/images/2022/img_0945.jpg)

Honestly, I don't miss it.

![](/assets/images/2022/img_0941.jpg)

But nor do I regret it. I've got all the trophies now and they can't take that away from me.

![](/assets/images/2022/img_0944.jpg)

Sure, I didn't include a screenshot of the platinum trophy in this post, but you believe me right?

![](/assets/images/2022/img_0942.jpg)

Cyberpunk 2077. Amazing! I never did work out who that guy was supposed to be? Is he the guy from NCIS: Los Angeles?

![](/assets/images/2022/img_0943.jpg)

We close this chapter of gaming and say our goodbyes. Cyberpunk 2077, you were a terrible game. I'd much rather play Horizon Zero Dawn. That game never crashed. Not even once. And it looks amazing.

![](/assets/images/2022/img_0946.jpg)

With that, we hold the buttons to end the Cyberpunk 2077 saga - Ctrl, Alt and...

![](/assets/images/2022/img_0947.jpg)

Del.
