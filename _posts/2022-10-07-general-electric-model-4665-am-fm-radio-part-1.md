There always needs to be a starting point. And this is where we are starting. An overview of the General Electric Model 4665 AM/FM Electronic Digital Clock Radio. Neat things about this radio is that it has a vacuum fluorescent display (VFD) for the clock section.

![](/assets/images/2022/img_0963.jpg)

VFD has always been an arch-nemesis of [my precious Nixie clock](/nixie-clock-slot-machine-demo-by-chronix/). I don't think the technology was that much of an improvement and quite frankly love the glow of the Nixie tubes. Well that and probably some influence from Stein's Gate c/o Nick. Yes that Nick. SuperNick.

![](/assets/images/2022/img_0964.jpg)

Overall though, this radio has held up quite well for the last 42 years. On closer inspection it turns out the date stamp I could find internally was 8004. Week 4, 1980. What a way to break in the 80s! I'm sure this jammed out a fair deal of [John Farnham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Farnham) back in the day. Imagine waking up to that every day of the week for 42 years. Pure bliss insanity.
