![A photo of Grevillea Juniperina Gold](/assets/images/2024/img_1282.jpg)

It's been almost a month since [I last posted](/the-garden-lizards-and-bees/) about the garden. I guess we're due for a bit of a dive into what I would populate my garden with if I had a garden I owned, not rented.

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1284.jpg)

Let me be clear, there would be no buffalo grass. Weeds would probably roam free and it would be glorious. At that point are they even weeds?

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1285.jpg)

But to help native creatures a little I'd probably install some sort of ground cover like spider flowers, [Grevilleas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grevillea).

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1283.jpg)

So there'd be a bunch of native ground cover in the form of Grevillea juniperina. Handsome looking devils, aren't they?

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1267.jpg)

I guess I'd probably hit up the local hardware store and buy a bunch of [bee hotels](https://entomologistlounge.wordpress.com/2017/09/18/insect-hotels-a-refuge-or-a-fad/) since my property was likely cleared by the previous owners.

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1268.jpg)

I have no idea where to find untreated wood so these would absolutely tide me, and the bees, over for a while.

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1270.jpg)

[Blue-banded bees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amegilla). We need purple and blue to bring them in. Let's load up with [lavender](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lavandula). It will grow above the ground cover quite well and flowers most of the year... I think.

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1269.jpg)

I mean we probably have a native variety. Maybe I should just get some native daisies from [Brachyscome spp.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brachyscome)

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1271.jpg)

I guess there's a fair bit, really not sure what I'd go for.

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1272.jpg)

Which brings us back around to grevilleas.

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1273.jpg)

I mean it doesn't really but here they are.

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1274.jpg)

So many types, I'd definitely want a prostrate type - meaning ground cover.

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1275.jpg)

But most of them are spiky...

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1276.jpg)

I would not want to fall into a grevillea bush.

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1277.jpg)

Or even a stand of grevillea! I guess I'd also like bottlebrush-type trees. Maybe some [Viola hederacea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viola_hederacea) or similar in the shaded areas.

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1278.jpg)

Weeping grass, [Microlaena stipoides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microlaena_stipoides), would be high up on my list for a lawn replacement. The above is definitely not weeping grass.

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1279.jpg)

Creeping Boobialla, [Myoporum parvifolium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myoporum_parvifolium) is hardy so might make the list if the grevillea doesn't outcompete it.

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1280.jpg)

I guess it's all just a pipe dream though. Who knows if we'll get another year on the lease. The uncertainty of housing is one of the most depressing things I get to think about.

![](/assets/images/2024/img_1281.jpg)

Maybe I'll just live in some broken ceramic pots - a potential lizard lounge maybe? This little lizard, a [bar-sided skink (Concinnia tenuis)](https://inaturalist.ala.org.au/taxa/538970-Concinnia-tenuis), has no trouble hunting bugs in the carrot planter! 🥰
